Master Budgets and Planning

The budgeting is done on multiple levels.
It enhances communication between management levels, and sets goals.

The budget, in which all other budgets are consolidated in, is called a master budget.

The Budget committee is the group of managers from all departments of the company that discuss the budgets, and make sure all of them are coordinated and realistic.

Budgeting is a bottom-up process.

A fixed budget is a budget that does not change.

A rolling budget is a budget that can be reviewed and reevaluated as needed.

The annual budget may be divided into smaller (eg. quarterly) budgets.

Capital Budgeting

Capital budgeting refers to analyzing alternative long-term investments and deciding which assets to acquire or sell.

  • Define task
  • Identify alternative actions

Relevant costs

  • Costs that are applicable to a particular application
  • Costs that Costs that are available
  • Costs that influence decisions
  • Future costs differ between alternatives

Sunk costs are the results of past decisions and can not change by any current or future decisions. They are irrelevant.

 

Out-of-Pocket costs are future outlays of cash associated with a decision. They are relevant.

 

Opportunity costs are the potential benefits given up to take the current decision. They are relevant.

Make or Buy decisions

Make or Buy decisions are decisions about whether to develop something in-house, or outsource it/buy it on the market.

  • Incremental costs need to be taken into account
  • Cost must include
    • Direct material costs
    • Direct labor costs
    • Incremental overhead
  • The predetermined overhead rate should not be used to determine the product cost

Scrap or Rework decisions

Scrap or Rework decisions are decisions on when to abandon a failed project or product, and when to try to save it.

 

As long as the rework costs are covered through the sale of the product, and rework does not interfere with normal production, it is worth it to rework the product.

Sell or Process decisions

Sell or Process decisions are decisions on whether to process the product into something more valuable, or to sell it at its normal price.
