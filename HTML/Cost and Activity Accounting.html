Step-wise costs

Step-wise costs are costs that are constant for in isolated regions, but on the boundaries of these regions they change value.

Measuring costs

  1. Scatter diagrams
    • Use proper scatterplots, don’t just draw a line through data like the prof did. Seriously, don’t do what the prof did in your thesis.
  2. High-low method
    • You take the highest and lowest datapoint and draw a line through both. Don’t use this. Ever.
  3. Least-square regression
    • Here you look at the line with the lowest average deviation from all points, and Excel does it for you automatically. This one you can actually use, it makes a lot of sense. Be sure to note your rate.

Master Budgets and Planning

The budgeting is done on multiple levels.
It enhances communication between management levels, and sets goals.

The budget, in which all other budgets are consolidated in, is called a master budget.

The Budget committee is the group of managers from all departments of the company that discuss the budgets, and make sure all of them are coordinated and realistic.

Budgeting is a bottom-up process.

A fixed budget is a budget that does not change.

A rolling budget is a budget that can be reviewed and reevaluated as needed.

The annual budget may be divided into smaller (eg. quarterly) budgets.

Types of budgets

  • Operating budget
    • Sales budget
      • Estimated unit sales
      • Estimated unit price
    • Cash budget
      • Actual columns
  • CapEx (capital expenditure)
  • Financial budget

Capital Budgeting

Capital budgeting refers to analyzing alternative long-term investments and deciding which assets to acquire or sell.
