Entity-Relationship Modeling

Ethical and Privacy Challenges

Ethics refers to the principles of right and wrong that individuals use to make choices that guide their behavior.

Ethical Frameworks and Standards

  • Utalitarian Approach
    • Greatest good & Least harm
  • Rights Approach
    • Protects and respects moral rights of all parties
  • Fairness Aproach
    • treating peaple equaly & if not possible at least fairly
  • Common good Approach
    • Haveing respect for all others in all societies for the walfare for everyone

5 Steps of the Framework

  1. Recognize an ethical issue
  2. Get the facts
  3. Evaluate alternative actions
  4. Make a decision and test it
  5. Act and reflect on the outcome

Corporate Code of Ethics: A code of ethics is a collection of principles intended to guide decision making by members of the organization.

Fundamental Principles of Ethics:

  • Responsibility
  • Liability
  • Accountability

Unethical Illegal

Advancements in IT Causing Ethical Problems:

  • Information gathering (Customer data, private data)
  • Electronic Surveilance & Monitoring(email, internet trafic,…)

Categories of Ethical Issues

  • Privacy
    • Personal (Being able to act free from instructions)
    • Information (When and to what extent information about you can be gathered and distributed)
  • Accuracy
  • Property
  • Accessibility

Privacy Codes and Policies are an organization’s guidelines for protecting the privacy of its customers, clients, and employees.

  • Opt-out-model: customer permits company to collect information until customer requests not to collect
  • Opt-in-model: customer prohibits organization to collect informationunless authorized

The Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)

SDLC is a structured framework that consists of sequential processes by which information systems are developed

Participants in the Development Projects:

  • Users
  • System analyst
  • Programmer
  • Technical Specialist
  • System Stakeholders

System investigation (Starting point of the process)

Feasibility Study

Options:
1.Do nothing
2.Modify or enhance the existing system
3.Develop a completely new system

Parts:

1.Technical feasibility
* use existing hardware/software/ communications or purchase new
2.Economic feasibility
* project is a financialrisk, existing time & money (ROI, breakeven)
3.Behavioral feasibility
* any human issues involved in the systems development

System Analysis

Information gathering Determine requirements for modifying existing system or developing a new system

Systems/ User Requirements Deliver user requirements report based on the analysis

System Design

Tech requirements must be frozen

Content of Report
  1. System inputs, outputs, user interfaces
  2. Hardware, Software, Telecommunications, Personnel, Procedures
  3. Blueprint of how to integrate the components
